The following model shows that five mutually intersecting tetrahedra can be inscribed in a dodecahedron. Any symmetries of the dodecahdron can be thought of as permutations of these five objects. A careful analysis shows that only the even permutations of the tetrahedra can be enacted in this manner. This establishes an isomorphism of the group of symmetries of the dodecahedron with the alternating group A_5
