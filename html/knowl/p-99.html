(3) Assume that \(f\) and \(g\) are both onto functions. Given \(c \in C\text{,}\), we must show that there exists an \(a \in A\) such that \((g \circ f)(a) = g(f(a)) = c\text{.}\). However, since \(g\) is onto, there is an element \(b \in B\) such that \(g(b) = c\text{.}\). Similarly, there is an \(a \in A\) such that \(f(a) = b\text{.}\). Accordingly, \begin{equation*} (g \circ f)(a) = g(f(a)) = g(b) = c. \end{equation*}
