We can use exponential notation for groups just as we do in ordinary algebra. If \(G\) is a group and \(g \in G\text{,}\), then we define \(g^0 = e\text{.}\). For \(n \in {\mathbb N}\text{,}\), we define \begin{equation*} g^n = \underbrace{g \cdot g \cdots g}_{n \; \text{times}} \end{equation*} and \begin{equation*} g^{-n} = \underbrace{g^{-1} \cdot g^{-1} \cdots g^{-1}}_{n \; \text{times}}. \end{equation*}
