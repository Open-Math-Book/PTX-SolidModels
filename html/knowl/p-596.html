Let \(G\) be a group and \(a\) be any element in \(G\text{.}\). Then the set \begin{equation*} \langle a \rangle = \{ a^k : k \in {\mathbb Z} \} \end{equation*} is a subgroup of \(G\text{.}\). Furthermore, \(\langle a \rangle\) is the smallest subgroup of \(G\) that contains~\(a\text{.}\).
