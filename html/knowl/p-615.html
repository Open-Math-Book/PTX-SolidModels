We wish to find the smallest integer \(m\) such that \(e = b^m = a^{km}\text{.}\). By PropositionÂ 4.0.12, this is the smallest integer \(m\) such that \(n\) divides \(km\) or, equivalently, \(n/d\) divides \(m(k/d)\text{.}\). Since \(d\) is the greatest common divisor of \(n\) and \(k\text{,}\), \(n/d\) and \(k/d\) are relatively prime. Hence, for \(n/d\) to divide \(m(k/d)\) it must divide \(m\text{.}\). The smallest such \(m\) is \(n/d\text{.}\).
