Theorem4.0.25

If \(z^n = 1\text{,}\) then the \(n\)th roots of unity are \begin{equation*} z = \cis\left( \frac{2 k \pi}{n } \right), \end{equation*} where \(k = 0, 1, \ldots, n-1\text{.}\) Furthermore, the \(n\)th roots of unity form a cyclic subgroup of \({\mathbb T}\) of order \(n\)
