Example4.0.28

We will compute \(271^{321} \pmod{ 481}\text{.}\) Notice that \begin{equation*} 321 = 2^0 +2^6 + 2^8; \end{equation*} hence, computing \(271^{321} \pmod{ 481}\) is the same as computing \begin{equation*} 271^{2^0 +2^6 + 2^8 } \equiv 271^{2^0} \cdot 271^{2^6} \cdot 271^{2^8} \pmod{481}. \end{equation*} So it will suffice to compute \(271^{2^i} \pmod{481}\) where \(i = 0, 6, 8\text{.}\) It is very easy to see that \begin{equation*} 271^{2^1} = \mbox{73,441} \equiv 329 \pmod{481}. \end{equation*} We can square this result to obtain a value for \(271^{2^2} \pmod{481}\text{:}\) \begin{align*} 271^{ 2^2} & \equiv (271^{ 2^1})^2 \pmod{ 481}\\ & \equiv (329)^2 \pmod{ 481}\\ & \equiv \mbox{108,241} \pmod{ 481}\\ & \equiv 16 \pmod{ 481}. \end{align*} We are using the fact that \((a^{2^n})^2 \equiv a^{2 \cdot 2^n} \equiv a^{ 2^{n+1} } \pmod{ n}\text{.}\) Continuing, we can calculate \begin{equation*} 271^{ 2^6 } \equiv 419 \pmod{ 481} \end{equation*} and \begin{equation*} 271^{ 2^8 } \equiv 16 \pmod{ 481}. \end{equation*} Therefore, \begin{align*} 271^{ 321} & \equiv 271^{ 2^0 +2^6 + 2^8 } \pmod{ 481}\\ & \equiv 271^{ 2^0 } \cdot 271^{ 2^6 } \cdot 271^{ 2^8 } \pmod{ 481}\\ & \equiv 271 \cdot 419 \cdot 16 \pmod{ 481}\\ & \equiv \mbox{1,816,784} \pmod{ 481}\\ & \equiv 47 \pmod{ 481}. \end{align*}
