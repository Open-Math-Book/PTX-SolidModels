Let \(G\) be a cyclic group and \(a \in G\) be a generator for \(G\text{.}\). If \(g\) and \(h\) are in \(G\text{,}\), then they can be written as powers of \(a\text{,}\), say \(g = a^r\) and \(h = a^s\text{.}\). Since \begin{equation*} g h = a^r a^s = a^{r+s} = a^{s+r} = a^s a^r = h g, \end{equation*} \(G\) is abelian.
