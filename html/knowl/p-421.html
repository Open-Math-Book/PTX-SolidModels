Let \begin{align*} 1 & = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & 0\\ 0 & 1 \end{pmatrix} \qquad I = \begin{pmatrix} 0 & 1\\ -1 & 0 \end{pmatrix}\\ J & = \begin{pmatrix} 0 & i\\ i & 0 \end{pmatrix} \qquad K = \begin{pmatrix} i & 0\\ 0 & -i \end{pmatrix}, \end{align*} where \(i^2 = -1\text{.}\). Then the relations \(I^2 = J^2 = K^2 = -1\text{,}\), \(IJ=K\text{,}\), \(JK = I\text{,}\), \(KI = J\text{,}\), \(JI = -K\text{,}\), \(KJ = -I\text{,}\), and \(IK = -J\) hold. The set \(Q_8 = \{\pm 1, \pm I, \pm J, \pm K \}\) is a group called the quaternion group. Notice that \(Q_8\) is noncommutative.
