Suppose we wish to show that \begin{equation*} 1 + 2 + \cdots + n = \frac{n(n + 1)}{2} \end{equation*} for any natural number \(n\text{.}\). This formula is easily verified for small numbers such as \(n = 1\text{,}\), 2, 3, or 4, but it is impossible to verify for all natural numbers on a case-by-case basis. To prove the formula true in general, a more generic method is required.
