Exercise25

Let \(a\) and \(b\) be elements in a group \(G\text{.}\) Prove that \(ab^na^{-1} = (aba^{-1})^n\) for \(n \in \mathbb Z\text{.}\)

Hint

\begin{align*} (aba^{-1})^n & = (aba^{-1})(aba^{-1}) \cdots (aba^{-1})\\ & = ab(aa^{-1})b(aa^{-1})b \cdots b(aa^{-1})ba^{-1}\\ & = ab^na^{-1}. \end{align*}
