To add two complex numbers \(z=a+bi\) and \(w= c+di\text{,}\), we just add the corresponding real and imaginary parts: \begin{equation*} z + w=(a + bi ) + (c + di) = (a + c) + (b + d)i. \end{equation*} Remembering that \(i^2 = -1\text{,}\), we multiply complex numbers just like polynomials. The product of \(z\) and \(w\) is \begin{equation*} (a + bi )(c + di) = ac + bdi^2 + adi + bci = (ac -bd) +(ad + bc)i. \end{equation*}
